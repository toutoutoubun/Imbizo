## What changed in this iteration

- The `Token` component is now a true accessible button. It is keyboard-focusable, exposes a complete ARIA label, supports `aria-pressed`, and activates with Enter or Space, in line with the accessibility commitments that follow from [PRINCIPLES.md Part V](../PRINCIPLES.md#part-v--the-political-economy-of-research-software).
- The six highlight modes are functional: language, 4-M type, switch type, trigger role, mixed-code span, and integration-score band. This implements the [Part III](../PRINCIPLES.md#part-iii--pluralism-and-refusal) principle that the same data must remain available through multiple theoretical lenses.
- The annotation pane now includes concord links, trigger role, mixed-code variety, integration-score transparency, user tags, and noun-class controls for isiZulu, isiXhosa, Sesotho, and Setswana.
- Keyboard shortcuts are wired for mouseless annotation work: Arrow keys move focus, Shift+Arrow extends selection, Alt+Arrow jumps language boundaries, Ctrl/Cmd+Arrow jumps switch points, `L` opens language, `M` opens 4-M, `T` toggles trigger role, Space toggles playback, Esc clears selection, Ctrl/Cmd+1..6 select highlight modes, and Ctrl/Cmd+/ cycles modes.
- The project now has internationalisation infrastructure through static bundled locale files. English and Japanese are populated; Afrikaans, isiZulu, isiXhosa, Sesotho, and Setswana are present as null-valued community-review stubs. This follows the decolonial posture in [PRINCIPLES.md Part IV](../PRINCIPLES.md#part-iv--licence-philosophy) by refusing remote translation services or synthetic completeness.
- MUI and Emotion dependencies were removed because the prototype uses Radix UI, Tailwind, and lucide-react. This supports the low-resource commitment in [PRINCIPLES.md Part I](../PRINCIPLES.md#part-i--foundational-principles).
- A provenance log strip is now part of the Annotation Editor. It keeps recent provenance visible during annotation work, connecting to [PRINCIPLES.md Part V](../PRINCIPLES.md#part-v--the-political-economy-of-research-software) on preserving researcher labour rather than hiding it.
- The annotation pane is resizable and stores its width in local storage under `imbizoCS.annotationPaneWidth`, so the prototype adapts better across laptop and large-monitor use without changing the visual language.
